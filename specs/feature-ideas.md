## 1. Pre-Session: Getting People to START

### Study Commitments/Pledges

- "I commit to studying for 2 hours today" (public declaration)
- Morning check-in: "What are you working on today?"
- Study pacts with friends: "We both commit to 3 hours this week"

### Smart Scheduling

- "You usually study at 2pm on Tuesdays - want to start a session?"
- Calendar integration (block study time, see it alongside meetings)
- "Your group is starting a session in 15 minutes"

### Accountability Check-ins

- Daily/weekly goal reminders
- "You're behind on your Data Structures goal - 5 hours needed this week"
- Friend nudges: "Sarah is studying now, join her?"

## Key Strategic Insights

### What Makes This Different from Simple Time Tracking

- **Accountability features** - Commitments, pledges, buddy system
- **Goal-oriented** - Not just logging hours, but working toward something
- **Social but not performative** - "We're in this together" not "look at me"
- **Quality over quantity** - Streaks and consistency matter more than hours

### Metrics That Matter

- **Primary:** % toward active goals
- **Secondary:** Study streak (days)
- **Tertiary:** Milestones earned (meaningful badges)
- **De-emphasized:** Total hours (show but don't highlight)

### Psychology to Leverage

- **Social facilitation** - People work harder when others are watching
- **Commitment device** - Public pledges increase follow-through
- **Progress tracking** - Visible progress is motivating
- **Streak maintenance** - Loss aversion keeps people coming back
- **Shared struggle** - Community around common challenges reduces performance anxiety
